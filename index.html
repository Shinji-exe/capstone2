Facts About the Great Outdoors!

The Himalayan Mountains

The Himalayas are the youngest mountain range on Earth, forming around 50 million years ago when the Indian and Eurasian tectonic plates collided. The Himalayas are still growing, adding 10 millimeters per year.

Yellowstone National Park

Home to Old Faithful, the most famous geyser in the world, Yellowstone also has over 500 active geysers.

Most Visited National Park

The most visited national park in the world in 2023 was Great Smoky Mountains National Park, with 13,297,647 visitors. The park is located on the border of North Carolina and Tennessee. It's known for its ancient mountains, plant and animal life, and historical remnants of Southern Appalachian mountain culture.

Parks play a crucial role in our communities and the environment, offering a wide range of benefits. They serve as vital green spaces where people can relax, exercise, and connect with nature, promoting mental and physical well-being. Parks provide a safe area for recreational activities like walking, jogging, biking, and playing, which helps improve overall health. Environmentally, parks help combat urban heat by reducing temperatures, filtering pollutants, and improving air quality. They act as natural habitats, supporting local wildlife and promoting biodiversity. Trees and plants in parks also help absorb carbon dioxide, contributing to climate change mitigation. Socially, parks are gathering spaces that foster a sense of community. They host events, encourage social interaction, and provide a venue for families and friends to spend quality time together. Parks also have economic benefits, often increasing property values in nearby areas and attracting tourism.
